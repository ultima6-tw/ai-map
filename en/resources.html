How to Choose an AI Tool?

The same service at different tiers can carry completely different risks. Use these principles to decide quickly.

✅

Relatively Safe

ChatGPT Team / Enterprise, Google Workspace (Gemini) — explicit commitment not to use your inputs for model training. Suitable for general work content.

⚠️ Commitments cannot be verified; sensitive data should still not be entered.

⚠️

Use with Caution

ChatGPT Free, Gemini Personal — your data may be used for training. Avoid entering any unpublished research content or personal information.

🚫

Recommended to Avoid

DeepSeek, Qwen, Kimi, Ernie Bot — subject to China's National Intelligence Law, which grants the government authority to access your data. Responses may be censored or biased.

Rule: Sensitive data (unpublished experimental results, personal information) should never be entered into any online service, regardless of tier.

Academic Journal AI Policies

Check each journal's policy before submitting. Below is a key comparison of APS and ACS policies.

APS (American Physical Society)

  • Must disclose AI use in the Cover Letter
  • Completely prohibits AI-generated images
  • AI cannot be listed as an author; authors bear full responsibility
  • Reviewers may not upload manuscripts to external AI services
🔗 APS Official Policy Page

ACS (American Chemical Society)

  • Must disclose AI use in the Acknowledgments
  • AI-generated images allowed but must be noted in the caption
  • AI cannot be listed as an author; authors bear full responsibility
  • Editors may reject manuscripts for "excessive" AI use
🔗 ACS Official Policy Page
Policies vary by journal. Always check the latest policy of your target journal before submission.
