LLM Fundamentals: How Does AI Actually Work?
From the relationship between AI / ML / LLM, to hallucinations, context windows, and the stateless nature of models — after this, you'll understand the root cause of all the strange behaviors.

What Is AI, ML, and LLM — and How Does an LLM Generate Text?

Using a Russian nesting doll analogy to clarify the nested relationship: AI → Machine Learning → Deep Learning → LLM. Then, the token prediction loop explains how LLMs generate text one word at a time — the core mechanism behind all AI behavior.

Chapter 2

Tokens and Temperature

LLMs don't see "words" — they see tokens. How are tokens billed? Why does Chinese cost more than English? And how does temperature control the randomness of AI outputs?

Chapter 3

The Hallucination Problem: Why Does AI Fabricate Papers?

AI picks the "statistically most likely token," not the "most correct one." These are completely different things — this is the root of hallucinations, and why all outputs must be verified.

🎬 Video Coming Soon
Chapter 4

Context Windows and Memory Limits

AI has a limited "working memory" called the context window. When the window is full, old content gets pushed out — which is why AI "forgets" things in long conversations.

🎬 Video Coming Soon
Chapter 5

The Truth About AI: It Is Stateless

The moment you press send, AI is "born" for that request and disappears after replying. The "conversation memory" you experience is an illusion created by the app re-packaging your chat history — this understanding explains the root of all strange behaviors.

Chapter 2

The Samsung Incident: Where Did the Data Go?

In 2023, Samsung engineers pasted confidential source code into ChatGPT, and the data was uploaded directly to OpenAI's servers. This isn't an isolated case — any time you use an online AI, your data leaves your computer. Samsung subsequently banned all external AI use.
