# AI Glossary

This page compiles core terms frequently used in the field of Artificial Intelligence to help you build a solid conceptual foundation.

---

## Infrastructure & Models

### **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**
A branch of computer science aimed at developing systems capable of simulating human intelligence, learning, reasoning, and problem-solving.

### **Machine Learning (ML)**
A subfield of AI that focuses on algorithms that learn patterns from vast datasets and make predictions without explicit instructions.

### **Deep Learning (DL)**
A subset of Machine Learning centered around "Neural Networks" that mimic brain structure. It is the primary technology behind modern AI breakthroughs like ChatGPT.

### **Neural Network (ANN)**
A structure composed of many digital "neurons." These neurons connect via "Parameters," simulating how the brain processes information.

### **Large Language Model (LLM)**
Deep learning models trained on massive text datasets. They understand and generate human language. Examples include GPT-4, Claude 3, and Llama 3.

---

## Key Technologies & Mechanisms

### **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**
A field focused on enabling computers to understand, interpret, generate, and process human language. LLMs represent the pinnacle of NLP.

### **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)**
A technique that combines LLMs with external knowledge bases (like internal company documents). It allows the AI to search for information before summarizing it, significantly reducing "Hallucinations."

### **Transformer Architecture**
The core architecture of modern LLMs. It introduced the "Attention Mechanism," which allows models to understand the context of words in a sentence.

### **Token**
The basic unit of text for an AI. AI doesn't read letters directly; it breaks text into Tokens. Roughly 1000 Tokens equals about 750 English words.

---

## Applications & Implementation

### **Prompt Engineering**
The skill of carefully designing inputs (prompts) to guide an LLM to produce more accurate and useful responses.

### **AI Agent**
An AI system with "thinking, decision-making, and acting" capabilities. It can use tools (like web browsing, coding, or emailing) to complete complex tasks autonomously.

### **Hallucination**
Occurs when an AI generates content that looks correct but is factually incorrect or entirely fabricated.
